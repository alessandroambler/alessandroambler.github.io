About

Hi! My name is Alessandro Ambler, and I completed my PhD in physics at McGill University under the supervision of Professor Brigitte Vachon. I worked on the ATLAS experiment at CERN's Large Hadron Collider, winner of the 2025 Breakthrough Prize in Physics. I graduated in 2024, and my thesis was awarded the Yablonovitch Research Prize for the best research accomplishment in experimental physics at McGill. I also volunteered as an instructor for the McGill particle physics masterclass. Here is a list of my publications.

Projects

Data Analysis : First Observation of a Physics Process

My PhD research focused on high-energy physics data analysis searching for new interactions. This work led to the first observation of the production of a W boson and two photons in proton-proton collisions, published by ATLAS. You can read more in the official ATLAS blog post that I co-wrote.

Signal Processing : Simulating and Optimizing Analog and Digital Signal Reconstruction

My MSc research focused on the development and simulation of analog and digital signal processing algorithms for energy reconstruction in the noisy environment of the ATLAS Liquid Argon Calorimeters. This was part of the Phase-II upgrade of the ATLAS Liquid Argon Calorimeter. In addition, during my PhD, I explored the use of machine learning (ML) in the form of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for use in real-time energy reconstruction. These CNNs used dilated convolutional layers inspired by Google DeepMind's WaveNet algorithm, which outperformed the current algorithms used in the ATLAS Calorimeter electronics.
