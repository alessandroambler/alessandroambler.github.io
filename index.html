About Me

Hi! My name is Alessandro Ambler, and I completed my PhD in physics at McGill University under the supervision of Professor Brigitte Vachon.

I worked on the ATLAS experiment at CERN's Large Hadron Collider, winner of the 2025 Breakthrough Prize in Physics. My research focused on high-energy physics data analysis searching for new interactions. This work led to the first observation of the production of a W boson and two photons in proton-proton collisions, published by ATLAS. You can read more in the official ATLAS blog post that I co-wrote.

In addition, I contributed to the development and simulation of analog and digital signal processing algorithms for the Phase-II upgrade of the ATLAS Liquid Argon Calorimeter. This included the exploration of machine learning in the form of convolutional neural networks for use in real-time energy reconstruction.

I graduated in 2024, and my thesis was awarded the Yablonovitch Research Prize for the best research accomplishment in experimental physics at McGill.

I also volunteered as an instructor for the McGill particle physics masterclass.
